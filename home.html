The Prophet ﷺ - An Introduction

"Muḥammad is the Messenger of Allah; and those with him are forceful against the disbelievers, merciful among themselves. You see them bowing and prostrating [in prayer], seeking bounty from Allah and [His] pleasure. Their sign is in their faces from the effect of prostration [i.e., prayer]. That is their description in the Torah..."

~ [48: 29]

Muhammad Ibn Abdulllah ﷺ is the last messenger of Allah, whose forethcoming has been foretold in major scriptures of monotheistic religions prior to Islam, namely: The Old Testament and The New Testament. In Islam, Muhammad ﷺ is the final messenger of Allah, sent to perfect moral character and finalize religion. He also recieved the Qur'an as divine revelation over a period of 23 years.

The Prophet ﷺ and his sunnah were the foundation for Islamic intellectualism that prevailed from his death to late 1500s. Although intellectualism still prevailed, even on to the contemporary world, the muslims during the specified time period were the ones who laid down the foundations of Islamic thought. Therefore, the prophet ﷺ not only served as a link between revelation and humanity, but also as an exegite of the Qur'anic verses. Through his explanations, the muslims were able to interpret the source of divine law, Qur'an. Hence, the Sunnah is now an important source of Islamic law and thought, second only to the Qur'an. It is important to know the Sunnah to understand Islamic intellectualism along with the Qur'an. Famous books of Sunnah include Sahih Bukhari, Sahih Muslim, and Sunan Abu Dawood.
